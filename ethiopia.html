Ethiopia

I traveled to Ethiopia in October 2018. It was my first, and up to today my only trip to an african country. I do not really remember why I was especially interested in Ethiopia, but the country has been a place I wanted to visit for a long time.
Like on the other trips, I didn't really plan anything before starting my trip and wasn't part of a tour. I think visiting Ethiopia without an organized tour was much harder than it was in any of the other countries I ever visited. Tourism is really not focused on backpacking tourists traveling alone and communication was difficult. However, the most beautiful and exciting things that happened to me during my trip would not have been possible with an organized tour, and the trip is not nearly as interesting and special with a layer of tour guides between you and the country and its people. Traveling in Ethiopia was sometimes very exhausting, but also very exciting.
The places I visited during my trip were Bahir Dar, Gonder, Simien National Park, Aksum, Abiy Addi, Hawzien, Lalibela, Awassa, Sodo, Debre Zeyit, Harar and Addis Abeba.
What I recall strongly in comparison to other countries I visited was how devided Ethiopia felt in different ways. The country is divided by religion (mainly Christianity and Islam), by language (approximately 90 different languages spoken) and by ethonology (more than 80 ethnic groups).
